Which UI direction feels right for NeuralShift AI?

Consider: you're an AI consultancy selling to AU SMBs. The site should convey trust, intelligence, and approachability — not intimidation.

A

Refined Current — Clean Up, Don't Rebuild

Keep the dark theme + glass morphism foundation. Strip back animation excess (particles, neural-pulse, gradient-shift all competing). Simplify to 2-3 hero animations max. Make glass cards crisper. Tighten typography hierarchy. Fix scroll performance.

Pros

  • Least work — refinement not rebuild
  • Existing brand identity preserved
  • Focus effort on scroll + audit enhancement

Cons

  • Dark + heavy animations can feel "techy intimidating" to SMB owners
  • Glass morphism is becoming dated (2021-2023 trend)
B

Modern Minimal — Light, Confident, Editorial

Shift to a light/neutral base with bold typography (Syne already supports this). Strategic dark sections for contrast. Subtle micro-interactions instead of particle effects. Think: Stripe, Linear, Vercel — companies that sell complex tech simply. Data visualizations replace particle backgrounds.

Pros

  • More approachable for non-technical SMB decision makers
  • Faster performance (fewer GPU-heavy effects)
  • Timeless — won't feel dated in 12 months
  • Better readability and content focus

Cons

  • Bigger visual overhaul
  • Needs careful execution to not feel "generic SaaS"
C

Hybrid — Dark Hero, Light Body

Keep the dark, impactful hero section (simplified — no particles, just clean gradients + strong typography). Transition to light/neutral for content sections (services, case studies, audit). Dark again for CTAs. This creates natural visual rhythm and reduces eye fatigue on long scrolls.

Pros

  • Best of both worlds — impact + readability
  • Natural scroll rhythm (dark → light → dark)
  • Hero keeps its punch, content sections breathe
  • Moderate effort — hero stays, body sections get lighter

Cons

  • Transition zones need careful design
  • Two "modes" to maintain
